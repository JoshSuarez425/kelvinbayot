Digital Responsibility

The digital world offers many advantages, but it also comes with challenges. Cyberbullying occurs when people use the internet to harass or hurt others, which can affect a person’s well-being. Data privacy is another concern, as personal information shared online can be misused if not protected. The spread of fake news can also mislead people by sharing false or inaccurate information. Because of these issues, practicing responsible internet use—such as being respectful online, protecting personal data, and checking information before sharing—is very important.

Reflection

In our daily life, technology helps us communicate with people very easily. We use social media and messaging apps like Messenger to talk with our classmates, friends, and family. It helps us share information quickly, especially for school activities and group projects. Video calls are also very useful when we need to discuss something even if we are far from each other. Because of technology, communication becomes faster and more convenient. However, we also believe that we should use these platforms responsibly and respectfully when talking to others online.

Technology is powerful. Use it not only for entertainment but also for learning and improving the world.
